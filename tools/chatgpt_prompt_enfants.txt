== WHAT IS A CONTRADICTION? ==

A contradiction is a verifiable factual inconsistency between two emails. There are two types:

TYPE 1 — intra-sender
Both emails are from the same person, and they say incompatible things.

Examples specific to enfants:
- She states in one email that she agreed to a custody schedule, then denies ever agreeing to it in a later email.
- She confirms a school enrolment decision, then later claims it was never discussed.
- She acknowledges receipt of child support payment, then later claims it was never paid.
- She commits to a Skype or video call schedule with the children, then later claims no such agreement existed.
- She gives a reason for refusing a holiday (e.g. school exams), but the same period was unproblematic in another email.
- She describes the children's living situation or health in contradictory ways across emails.

TYPE 2 — cross-sender
One email is from "Moi" and one from "Ex-femme", where one directly and factually refutes a specific claim made by the other.

Examples specific to enfants:
- He documents an agreed holiday or access arrangement in writing; she later denies it existed.
- She claims he never exercised a right; his email proves he requested it and she refused.
- She asserts the children expressed a preference; he quotes a direct contradiction from an earlier exchange.


== WHAT IS NOT A CONTRADICTION ==

Do NOT flag these:
- A simple change of opinion or preference over time (unless the person denies having held the earlier position)
- Disagreements about interpretation or feelings
- One person's account vs. the other's when both are subjective views
- Incomplete information or omissions (unless they directly negate a prior claim)
- Different levels of detail between emails
- Normal evolution of children's schooling, activities or health over time


== SEVERITY SCALE ==

high   : Clear lie, direct perjury risk, or a factual claim that is verifiably false based on the other email. Could be directly cited in court. (e.g. denying an agreed custody arrangement that is documented in writing)
medium : Significant discrepancy in facts, dates, commitments, or parental agreements. Raises serious credibility issues. (e.g. contradictory accounts of a school decision or holiday refusal)
low    : Minor inconsistency or change of position. Worth noting but not directly harmful. (e.g. a school name or minor schedule detail that changed without explanation)

When in doubt, prefer medium over high. Only use high when the contradiction is unambiguous and legally significant.
