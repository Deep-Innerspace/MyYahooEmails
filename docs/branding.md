# Northline — Brand Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Approved direction  
**Primary slogan:** **Clarity under pressure**  
**Category:** Divorce intelligence platform

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Northline** is a brand for people navigating contested or high-conflict divorce. It helps users turn years of correspondence into structured evidence, clear timelines, surfaced contradictions, and calmer, safer replies.

Northline is built for moments when facts are buried, communication is adversarial, and emotional pressure can lead to costly mistakes. The brand promise is not abstract healing. It is clear thinking, steady control, and protection when the situation is at its most difficult.

### Brand lockup

**Northline**  
**Clarity under pressure**

## 4. Brand Strategy

### The problem Northline solves

During divorce, especially high-conflict divorce:
- records become overwhelming
- timelines are hard to reconstruct
- important contradictions are easy to miss
- users become reactive under emotional pressure
- poorly phrased responses can be used against them

Northline exists to reduce noise, restore order, and help users act from clarity rather than emotion.

### The transformation

**From:**
- buried in years of correspondence
- unclear about what matters
- reactive, drained, exposed
- uncertain how to answer safely

**To:**
- supported by an organized record
- clear on facts, patterns, and chronology
- calmer and more strategic
- able to answer with composure and control

### Brand territory

**Clarity under pressure**

This is the core brand territory and the central filter for all messaging, design, and product experience.

## 5. Audience

### Primary audience

People going through a contested, adversarial, or emotionally exhausting divorce who need:
- structure
- evidence support
- communication help
- confidence in what to document and how to respond

### Secondary audience

- lawyers or legal advisors using client-prepared materials

Users are often:

- afraid of missing important proof

## 8. Tone of Voice

### Voice principles

Northline speaks with:
- precision, without coldness
- calm, without passivity
- authority, without intimidation
- empathy, without sentimentality

### Tone characteristics

- clear
- measured
- grounded
- disciplined
- humane
- non-reactive

### Tone rules

#### Do
- write short, clean, confident sentences
- use concrete, useful language
- emphasize clarity, structure, and control
- acknowledge stress without dramatizing it
- sound composed even when the subject is intense

#### Do not
- romanticize divorce
- use self-help clichés
- overuse legal jargon
- sound emotional, righteous, or activist
- sound like a surveillance tool
- sound manipulative or adversarial

### Tone examples

**Weak:**  
“Find your way toward the light after your darkest chapter.”

**Strong:**  
“Keep the facts clear. Keep your responses controlled.”

**Weak:**  
“We help you heal from difficult emotional transitions.”

**Strong:**  
“We help you organize the record and respond with clarity.”

## 11. Visual Identity

### Design principles

The visual system should communicate:
- calm control
- orientation and direction
- clarity through structure
- reduced noise
- confidence without aggression

### Symbol direction

Preferred symbol direction:
- eye contour formed by simple lines
- central upward arrow as pupil
- evokes vision, direction, future orientation, and a stable point of reference

The symbol should communicate:
- seeing clearly
- holding a line
- guidance without overstatement
- controlled focus

### Logo behavior

The logo should feel:
- simple
- legible at small sizes
- modern
- professional
- recognizably distinct from legal-tech clichés

Avoid:
- gavels
- scales of justice
- hearts
- doves
- flames, storms, or overt drama
- overly literal legal iconography

## 12. Color System

Northline colors should feel composed, credible, and high-contrast without becoming harsh.

### Primary palette

#### 1. Northline Navy
- **Hex:** `#1E3557`
- **Role:** core brand color, trust, seriousness, depth

#### 2. Graphite
- **Hex:** `#44515F`
- **Role:** structure, secondary text, interfaces, icon contour

#### 3. Soft Ivory
- **Hex:** `#F5F2EA`
- **Role:** light backgrounds, contrast balance, editorial calm

#### 4. Signal Amber
- **Hex:** `#D6A14B`
- **Role:** accent, focal point, arrow/pupil, highlight moments

### Secondary neutrals

#### Slate Mist
- **Hex:** `#6B7785`

#### Deep Ink
- **Hex:** `#142337`

#### Stone
- **Hex:** `#C8C2B6`

### Color usage rules

- Navy is the anchor.
- Ivory is the breathing space.
- Graphite provides structure and restraint.
- Amber must be used sparingly to create focus and recall.
- Avoid large warm gradients, bright greens, or overly optimistic palettes.
- The palette should feel stable, not cheerful.

### Recommended color balance

- 55% Navy / Deep Ink
- 25% Ivory
- 15% Graphite / Slate
- 5% Amber

## 13. Typography

Typography should balance authority and usability.

### Primary brand typeface

**Instrument Serif**  
Use for:
- brand wordmark
- hero headlines
- editorial emphasis
- selected campaign headlines

### UI and functional typeface

**Inter**  
Use for:
- interface text
- navigation
- body copy
- labels
- buttons
- captions

### Optional premium editorial alternative

**Canela** or **Cormorant Garamond**  
Only for high-end editorial or campaign assets, not for product UI.

### Typography rules

- Use serif sparingly and strategically.
- The serif should signal dignity, not nostalgia.
- Use sans-serif for most communication and all product surfaces.
- Maintain generous spacing and clean hierarchy.
- Avoid overly condensed or futuristic display fonts.

### Suggested hierarchy

#### H1
Instrument Serif, large, confident, generous spacing

#### H2
Inter or Instrument Serif depending on context

#### Body
Inter Regular

#### Labels / UI
Inter Medium

## 14. Iconography

### Style

- minimal line-based icons
- geometric, clean, restrained
- clear at small sizes
- no cartoon style
- no decorative excess

### Icon theme

Icons should reflect:
- structure
- search
- references
- documents
- timelines
- controlled communication
- insight

### Avoid

- playful rounded icon sets
- pictograms that feel consumer-social
- aggressive shapes or warning-heavy design language

## 15. Imagery

### Imagery direction

Northline should avoid cliché divorce imagery.

Avoid:
- arguing couples
- crying people
- courtrooms with gavels
- dramatic storms or broken objects
- hands on faces
- generic “stress at desk” stock photos

Use instead:
- calm editorial portraits
- structured close-ups of documents, notes, screens, lines, margins, markers
- abstract compositions that suggest order emerging from complexity
- quiet, modern workspaces
- subtle directionality, lines, grids, or references

### Mood

The image world should feel:
- still
- intentional
- premium
- intelligent
- controlled

## 18. Writing Style Guide

### Brand writing rules

- favor directness over cleverness
- favor clarity over metaphor
- favor concrete benefit over emotional flourish
- use short paragraphs
- keep language intelligible for stressed users
- never make the user feel judged or passive

### Sentence style

Preferred:
- concise
- active voice
- useful
- measured

### Example

**Bad:**  
“Our platform empowers individuals to navigate their emotional and procedural journey with resilience.”

**Good:**  
“Northline helps you organize the record and respond with control.”

## 20. Brand Do / Don't

### Do
- make the brand feel serious, stable, and intelligent
- communicate usefulness fast
- show calm control in every touchpoint
- use the amber accent as a precise focal marker
- keep the messaging grounded in real user pressure

### Don't
- over-poeticize the brand
- drift into wellness or therapy language
- look like a generic B2B legal-tech startup
- sound combative or revenge-driven
- overload surfaces with text, motion, or decoration

---

## 21. Accessibility and Inclusivity

Northline is an international product. The brand system must support:
- high legibility
- multilingual usage
- low-cognitive-load messaging
- strong contrast ratios
- clear hierarchy for stressed users
- neutral, inclusive language

### Accessibility rules

- maintain accessible color contrast
- avoid low-contrast amber on ivory for body text
- do not rely on color alone to communicate status
- keep headings and buttons easy to scan
- prioritize plain language over jargon
